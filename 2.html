How did we start as an advocacy group?

It started when I saw a post through social media where children in rural areas had to walk several kilometers just to arrive at their school. Some of them even carried heavy bags and I saw that their classrooms were small and lacked resources. It really struck me because it made me realize how different their situation is compared to mine; I have an easier access to education with learning materials and even books. It’s quite unfair that education is still difficult to access for some students. It made me reflect that we take these opportunities for granted. From then on, I wanted to do something, even in a small way, to raise awareness about this issue. Then, I came up with an idea of using a website as a platform for my advocacy: to inform people with this issue. With my website, I aim to educate everyone about education inequality, inspire others to care about the issue, and to make an impact on everyone.

Who am I?

Hello! I’m Ahzeah Joshel E. Walan and I’m a student who values the importance of education. I’ve experienced how having access to learning resources and guidance can make studying easier, but I also realized that not all students are given the same opportunities. This motivated me to create an advocacy website focused on giving everyone a quality access to education. Through this platform, I hope to raise awareness and inspire others to support a quality education for all.
