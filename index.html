Background of the Problem

Quality education is a fundamental process that develops the whole child, focusing on more than just academics. It involves equipping students with knowledge, skills, and values to make meaningful contributions to society. However, despite global efforts and initiatives, the problem of quality education remains a pressing concern. Millions of young children do not have access to education, and if they do, the quality of learning is either competent or poor and insufficient. Several issues contribute to this problem. In many developing countries, schools have overcrowded classrooms, lack of learning materials and technology, and shortage of trained teachers. According to UNESCO, 272 million children and youth are still out of school in 2025, while others who enrolled struggle with numeracy skills and basic literacy. Without addressing these issues, students risk being unprepared for future challenges, limiting their chances for employment and social mobility.

What is being done about the problem?

Quality education remains a pressing issue, but there are numerous ways to address it. The international organizations play a significant role in this regard: the United Nations promotes Sustainable Development Goal 4, which seeks to ensure a quality education for all. UNESCO also contributed through programs such as the Global Education Monitoring (GEM) Report and training teachers, while UNICEF focuses on reaching marginalized children, supporting digital learning, and making a child-friendly environment. The World Bank and other organizations provide funding for educational reforms and infrastructure development to improve education quality worldwide. At the national and local levels, the governments, communities also take action. Governments implement education sector reforms, update curriculum, offer scholarships and financial aid, invest in classrooms, digital tools, and internet access. Volunteer tutoring is also provided to expand digital education. Collaboration among international bodies, governments, and communities is crucial for ensuring quality education is accessible to all.

Where do we come in?

In spite of all the efforts the government and communities made, there remain gaps that exist. EduBloom attempts to fill these gaps by acting as a platform where individuals can become educated, take action, and assist. Through creating awareness, it provides knowledge of the global and local education issues and features stories that show the need for quality education. EduBloom also encourages action by giving users to give, volunteer, take part, and sponsor education programs. In addition to awareness and resources, EduBloom also builds community through interactive platforms for collaboration, bridging volunteers, teachers, and supporters from across the globe. In this way, it supplements existing global and national efforts by catalyzing individual action and engaging audiences that conventional organizations can't readily touch, e.g., youth and internet communities. By acting as a center for advocacy for education, EduBloom raises awareness, participation, and overall effect in helping ensure that initiatives to further quality education reach more individuals.
